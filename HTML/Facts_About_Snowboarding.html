5 Facts About Snowboarding

  1. Snowboarding wasn't the original name for the sport. Snowboarding was originally called 'snurfing', which was a mix between the snow and surfing!

  2. The United States has the has the most Olympic Snowboarding medals. To Date, the U.S. has taken home 31 medals (14 of those gold). The country with the second most is Switzerland with just 13 medals.

  3. Less than 1% of snowboarders suffer injuries. This is crazy to think about especially due to the sport appearing so dangerous. The most common injury for snowboarders is a sprained wrist!

  4. Snowboarding was briefly outlawed in the U.S. in the 1980's. In the 1980's, Snowboarding was labeled as a dangerous teen craze that attracted youths onto unstable slopes and was promptly banned by all U.S. resorts.

  5. The record for the most flips done on a single jump is 4. Mark McMorris accomplished a spectacular feat by pulling off four flips in a single jump, This feat has only been repeated by eight other snowboarders.
